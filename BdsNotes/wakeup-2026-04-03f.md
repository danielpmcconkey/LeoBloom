### Architectural decisions
- Service placed in `LeoBloom.Dal` instead of `LeoBloom.Ledger` (BRD said Ledger). Reason: LeoBloom.Ledger project doesn't exist in the solution (only phantom obj dirs). Service needs Dal's ConnectionString and Repository — putting it in a separate project would require either circular deps or extracting interfaces. Dal is the right home until the project grows.
- TickSpec limitation: multi-Then scenarios break because each Then does `finally cleanup` which disposes the connection before the next Then runs. Fix: one compound Then per scenario. This is the pattern going forward for behavioral tests.
- `EntryType.Debit`/`Credit` collides with `NormalBalance.Debit`/`Credit` in F#. Must fully qualify as `EntryType.Debit` in any context where both are in scope. Same issue hit in 029 with `InstanceStatus.Confirmed` vs `TransferStatus.Confirmed`.

## Lessons learned this session
- TickSpec `Table.Rows` is `string[][]`, not named-access. Use `colIndex` helper.
- Npgsql nullable params: use `DBNull.Value`, not `Option.defaultValue null |> box`.
- F# DU case collision: always qualify when `EntryType` and `NormalBalance` share case names.
- One Then per scenario in TickSpec. Multi-Then breaks cleanup. Compound assertions are the pattern.
- Pat is a reusable audit agent. Blueprint at `Projects/Project005-PostJournalEntry/pat-audit-prompt.md` — generalize for future use.
